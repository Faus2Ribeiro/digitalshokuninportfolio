3D Work

I started my 3D adventures back at the beginning of 2016. It all started with a small pen and from there, it grew into trying to replicate reality or bringing sketch ideas to a more interactive field.

On this page, you will find some of the projects that I like to highlight. You can also check out my full collection on the button below!

Dust Tech Gear

This was my last big project in college. I had to create a sort of shelter to be used in 2050. In this project I studied Speculative Design and studied how it should be adapted in the future. I worked with three other people in this project, so shoutout too Foxie, Miguel, and Sofia for the great collaboration!

Small Japanese Style Apartment

This is one of my most recent side projects. I love to collect reference images of spaces to draw, and I've been collecting photographs of some Japanese Model Houses. So I got to try and make my own Japanese Style Model Apartment in Fusion.

Presidency Room of Lisbon Education College

This was a submmission for a project for the Lisbon Education College. We had to remodel this room for a more convenient use. This was a group project, so a huge thank you to my partners Foxie and okgeraldo for the collaboration!

  • Gallery

Tower Bench of Banco de Benfica

In Lisbon Education School there's a reocurring project called Banco de Benfica where the students are subjected to create a bench that represents the area of Benfica. I decided to focus on the beautiful Monsanto Park and it's mysterious towers.
At the end of the projects, the students can print out there benches in Benfica's FabLab and even upload their benches into open source online platforms like Wikifactory.

EasierWax, A Woman's Epilator

For this project, I had to create a woman's epilator following the Ten Principles of Good Design by Dieter Rams. It was quite challenging, but alongside my partner, we came up with a user friendly sleek design for a wireless epilator, with a portable charger that includes dispensers for the wax slabs. Huge thank you to my friend João for the partnership!

Olive Oil Bottle

I created an olive oil bottle to submit for a contest back in 2018. This was a small project I did to test out materials on AutoCAD. Fast forward a few years later, I decided to remaster it on Fusion360.
